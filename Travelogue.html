Travelogue

I strongly believe that travel broadens the mind. Not that I have circumnavigated the earth, but I have been to a couple of places. It started with Singapore
Singapore (1989) - Finally, I’m abroad
and Malaysia
Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia (1989) - I AM NOT STARING!
in 1989. After a long hiatus, Amma and I went to Sri Lanka
Negombo, Sri Lanka (2002) - Creepy Halo
in 2001. We later followed up with Dubai
Dubai, United Arab Emirates (2003) - I’ll quit when you do!
for the shopping festival in 2002. Later in 2003, I went to Germany to study (really?)
Nürburg, Germany (2006) - The Beer Bong - One liter of beer in 10 seconds flat. I also got to see Metallica, later that day
and wanted to see Europe in all its splendor and glory. From here I managed to visit France
Paris, France (2004) - First time in Paris with Amma

Venice, Italy (2004) - Quick! Someone give me a gun
, Vatican
Vatican City (2004) - C’mon! THIS IS THE WORLD’S SMALLEST COUNTRY
and Ireland
Limerick, Republic of Ireland (2005) - This is a beautiful town in the mid-west of the Republic of Ireland. The Cliffs of Moher are like a 2 hour drive from here
. Later, when I was in Madras during the summer of 2006, we (Amma, Chitti, Sahana and I) visited Hong Kong
Hong Kong (2006) - 3 Children and 1 Adult
, Thailand
Pattaya, Thailand (2006) - OK..that’s a dude

Shenzhen, China (2006) - Wait...that’s not what I meant by a turtle neck

Having lived in 3 different countries for a significant amount of time, I wanted to make the best use of it. Starting with India, I have been to most of the big cities. The earliest I remember was Bangalore
Look how thin I was
. It felt like a whole new country. More recently the guys and I went to Kodaikanal
Karthik really thinks he’s wikipedia personified
and later I took Xiaojie to Goa
Well red, perhaps?

In 2003, I moved to Germany and I think this is where I really started to get around. One of my very first trips was to Dresden with Dr. Govindan, his family and Muthu. I remember this vividly as this where I first saw snow
Kinderjoy
. From Dresden, Muthu and I visited Berlin as well. By the beginning of the second semester, our department arranged for a class trip to the island of Sylt. We were supposed to observe the morphology of the islands. So they said. But boy, did we have a good time
When life gives you lemons..
! Meanwhile, Amma visited me in Germany and we went to Munich
I have nothing to say about this picture
, Dresden
Looks like the guy is doing something else

In July 2006, I moved to China
I know..
lock, stock and barrel. Beijing was much more developed than I imagined. The sky was never blue though. Anyway, a month later, Xiaojie’s colleagues took us on a short trip to Bashang. This was a “grassland” about 3 hours away from Beijing. It was so much fun
That’s what I’m saying
. During the national holidays in October, Xiaojie, her colleagues and I traveled to Hangzhou
Blood, sweat and tears
, a beautiful city close to Shanghai. I had always wanted to see Shanghai and we could not then. On December 31st 2007, when Xiaojie and I were thinking how to spend New Year’s Eve, I suggested Shanghai
Happy New Year
. Within a few minutes we booked the flight tickets and a room at the Hilton, and were there in 5 hours! We spent the New Year in style.

I dream of visiting Antarctica some day and I’d also like to retrace the Silk Route. Nevertheless, of all my travels, I have 2 favorites - “The Trip” and “The Trip 2”.

The Trip


I call it simply The Trip. It was my first ever backpacking trip and I did it all alone. I had saved some money and was eager to see Europe. I started planning by the end of October 2004 and decided I was going to take the trains with an Inter-Rail pass. The route
The Trip
was set and I started off from Kiel in December 2004.

The first leg of my journey was from Paris. I had an overnight train
Singin’ in the train
to Girona
Gateway to Spain
(near Barcelona). It was nice to be in Barcelona
One of my best photos
, although I had not realized how difficult it would be without speaking Spanish! From here, I moved to Madrid
Not that I knew what it was
and also got to check out Toledo
Quite sunny
and Segovia
I learned something about myself that day
. Pablo, a colleague from CORELAB, had suggested I check Sevilla out. Went there and thought there was nothing significant. How wrong was I? There it was. An orange tree! I had never seen one before. The place where I am from had a lot of others (Mangoes, Coconut) but oranges? Pablo was right. When I look at the photos today, they look more like Clementine
The tree
. May be, but who cares?

From here I went to Lisbon
War memorial of a Sword
. It was surreal. I had not realized that not talking to anyone for a few days, made me want to implode. What was I thinking when I started without learning a few words in Spanish / Portuguese? Anyway, Lisboa was superb. Blue sky, cheap wine and nice food. By now it was the 24th of December and I had to get to Redondela
Heaven on earth
. Pablo had invited me to his place there for Christmas. It was heaven on earth. The view from his room was fantastic. His mom had prepared a great lunch
Sea food galore
but there was a little problem. See, back then I did not eat sea food. The poor thing felt bad (I was worse) and quickly fried some potatoes and eggs.

My next stop was San Sebastian, a beautiful coastal town in Spain close to the French border. Well, I was there in December’s winter and did not care much for it until I saw the beach. Inspired me to make a panorama
My first pano ever
of it. My next stop was Paris where Sebastien, another colleague, invited me over. His parents had a nice house in the suburbs. Stayed there for 2 days, had nice food
Bon appetit
and then moved on to Luxembourg.

I met with Dr. Govindan, a friend from Kiel, and his family in Lux
In Lux
and from there we went to Brussels
Bruxelles
. I have to say his place in Germany, was my home away from home. Anyway, I then wanted to go to Holland and I again lodged at Sebastien’s. He was doing his Master’s thesis there and had rented an apartment. From here I went to Rotterdam
Hobo in Rotterdam
and Amsterdam before heading back home. What a trip it was!
The Trip 2


Having had a great trip in 2004, I wanted to do something similar again. I started planning by October 2005 and wanted to cover as much as possible using the Inter-Rail. This time however I wanted to visit Egypt apart from covering northern and southern Europe
The Trip 2
. A special thanks to Xiaojie who traveled to Hamburg to get me my visa.

I started off from Kiel at around 5:00 AM on a Saturday and took a weekend ticket. In Germany, with this ticket one can take all “normal” trains without paying extra. I think I made the best use of this ticket by getting all the way to Munich. I remember changing 7 trains to get there and you can imagine how exhausting that would have been. Having reached Munich by around 19:00, I lodged at Ashutosh’s (from my University who later moved to Munich) place. I was pretty excited about my flight to Cairo 2 days later.

I reached Cairo
HEYYYY...CAN YOU HEAR ME
late at night and was glad to see someone from the youth hostel waiting to pick me up. Cairo seemed so much like India but the roads were better. People often asked where I was from and constantly mentioned “Amitabh Bachchan”. He’s a phenomenon there. I loved the food, especially kushari
Inexpensive and was a staple
, and was pigging out after eating European food for a year. I visited Aswan from there and cruised with Captain Ruby
The captain, Petty Officer and I
on the Nile. After spending about 5 days in Egypt I took a flight back to Munich.

Back in Germany, Ashutosh and I visited Salzburg
Ashutosh in Salzburg
and later I left for Karlsruhe from Munich. I stayed at Ajay’s (cousin) for about 4 days. You cannot imagine how much I was craving for home-cooked-south-Indian-food
Hungry this very moment
. Viji and Ajay were the perfect hosts and put up with me for 3 days. I traveled to Zurich
On this day I learned Zurich was not the capital city
and Bern from here before heading back to Kiel for Christmas.

From Kiel, I traveled north
This train was actually ON a ferry. Nenja Nakkitaan
and reached Malmo via Copenhagen. A friend’s friend was supposed to pick me up from the station. Keep in mind it was winter and I was in a different country. As luck would have it, this guy never showed up! I waited for 2 hours before checking myself in at a hotel. From here I wanted to get to a town called Bodoe in Norway. Bodoe
I guess it was around 8 AM
is situated just above the Arctic Circle and I simple wanted to cross that. I reached Bodoe at 06:00 and stayed there till 18:00. From here I got back to Oslo
See..I do not care for your tantrum
, spent a day and headed to Stockholm
Happy New Year
for the New Year. From here, I took a ferry to Helsinki
Duh..That’s not the ferry
. I retraced my route and reached Copenhagen
I had just lost €100
before heading back home after 30 days.
